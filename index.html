About

I’m a PhD student in Computer Science at the Federal University of Minas Gerais (UFMG), in Brazil, specializing in Information Retrieval. I am advised by Professor Rodrygo Santos.


My research focuses on Biomedical Information Retrieval, with a particular interest in using large language models (LLMs) to extract relevant passages and documents from medical corpora, given information needs expressed in natural language.


In 2020, I had the opportunity to work as a visiting research student at the University of Glasgow in Scotland (UK). During this period, in collaboration with Professors Craig MacDonald and Iadh Ounis, we produced key results for my PhD research, which were reported at SIGIR and TREC.

Education

I earned my Master's Degree (2017) in Computer Science from UFMG, under the supervision of Professor Berthier Ribeiro-Neto and the co-supervision of Professor Nivio Ziviani. My thesis focused on assessing the reputation of academic entities, such as research conferences, journals, and scientific articles.


I hold a Bachelor's Degree (2012) in Computer Science from Universidade de São Paulo (USP).
